About

I’m a systems neuroscientist studying how the brain transforms visual information into perception, navigation, and decision. My work bridges large-scale neural recordings with computational modeling to uncover how distributed circuits communicate. Ultimately, I aim to build an artificial brain that mirrors the full functional complexity of the human one — a system that truly thinks, sees, and adapts as biology does.

    Nov 2025: Postdoc-ing: my work combines large-scale Neuropixels recordings, visual flow stimuli, and computational modeling to reveal how circuits encode motion, space, and perception.

    March 2023: Left Cedars-Sinai, joined Dr. Tao & Li’s Lab in USC.

    Jan 2021: Officially earned my PhD — with an unexpected 1.5-year home extension courtesy of Covid Nineteen.

Schizophrenia risk-gene Crmp2 deficiency causes precocious critical period plasticity and deteriorated binocular vision

Effie, Science Bulletin, (2021).
Paper Full List
Abstract

In this study, by examining the classical experience-dependent OD plasticity of developing mouse V1, we revealed a new role of schizophrenia-risk gene Crmp2 in regulating the timing of CP for neuronal circuit refinement and maturation during postnatal development.

Portrait of visual cortical circuits for generating neural oscillation dynamics

Effie, Cognitive Neurodynamics, Review Paper (2020).
Paper
Abstract

The review summarizes recent experimental findings on elucidating cellular or circuitry mechanisms for the generation of neural oscillations with distinct rhythms in either developing or matured mouse V1, mainly focusing on visual relaying circuits and distinct local inhibitory circuits.
